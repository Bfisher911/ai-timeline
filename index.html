The Evolution of Artificial Intelligence

Early Symbolic AI
AI Winter Periods
Statistical ML
Deep Learning Era
1943

McCulloch & Pitts Neural Network

First mathematical model of a brain cell that could compute logical functions

1950

Turing Test Proposed

Shifted focus from "Can machines think?" to observable behavior

1956

Dartmouth Workshop

Term "Artificial Intelligence" coined; field formally established

1966

ELIZA Chatbot

Revealed human tendency to attribute understanding to simple pattern-matching

1973

"Lighthill Report"

Triggered UK funding cuts, contributing to first AI Winter

1980

Expert Systems Emerge

MYCIN, DENDRAL showed promise of rule-based knowledge representation

1987

First AI Winter Deepens

Expert systems proved costly to maintain and brittle outside narrow domains

1997

Deep Blue Defeats Kasparov

Chess champion falls to specialized computation, marking symbolic AI victory

2005

Statistical Methods Rise

Machine learning begins outperforming rule-based systems in many applications

2012

AlexNet & ImageNet

Deep learning dramatically improves computer vision, revitalizing neural networks

2014

GANs Introduced

Generative models enable AI to create realistic synthetic images

2017

Transformer Architecture

"Attention is All You Need" paper revolutionizes sequence modeling

2020

GPT-3 Demonstrates Scale

175 billion parameters show that massive models exhibit emergent capabilities

2022

Stable Diffusion & ChatGPT

Generative AI enters mainstream awareness and application

2023+

Multimodal Models Emerge

Systems integrate text, vision, audio in single architecture
